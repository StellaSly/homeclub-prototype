## 3. THE COMPETITIVE LANDSCAPE

### Direct loyalty rivals in SA hardware/DIY
**There are none of substance.** Builders Warehouse has a basic credit-account-linked rewards mechanism, Cashbuild has none worth naming. The DIY/home improvement category in SA has **no Discovery-grade loyalty programme**. Whoever builds the first proper one owns the category.

### The SA loyalty gold standard (the bar Naresh is benchmarking against)
- **FNB eBucks** — won 6 awards at the 2025 SA Loyalty Awards; gold standard for tiered rewards tied to financial behaviour.
- **Discovery Vitality** — gold standard for **behaviour change** (health) tied to rewards across multiple partners (Pick n Pay, Woolworths, Dis-Chem, Apple, Garmin etc.). This is the model Naresh referenced.
- **Clicks ClubCard** — most-used SA loyalty programme (80% of economically active SA), proves cashback-style mechanics dominate.
- **Checkers Xtra Savings** — instant-rewards leader; second most used.
- **Capitec Live Better** — most-loved by mass market; coalition-style cashback.
- **Pick n Pay Smart Shopper / Woolworths WRewards / Dis-Chem Benefit / Momentum Multiply** — all coalition models gaining ground.

### Truth & BrandMapp 2025/6 Loyalty Whitepaper — facts to weaponise in our pitch
- **85% of SA adults actively use loyalty programmes** (up from 82% the year prior).
- Average South African is now in **10.4 programmes** (up from 4.6 in 2015 — a 228% increase in 10 years).
- **77% say loyalty programmes shift where they buy groceries; 32% for clothes; 30% for health.** No DIY data point exists yet — meaning the category is virgin territory for behaviour shaping.
- **Cashback is king** — but consumers are increasingly programme-fatigued, so emotional and experiential differentiation matters.
- **73% still prefer a physical card** over an app — counterintuitive insight that means our digital design must include card-equivalent elegance (e.g. wallet pass, NFC tap-on-phone).

### TLC Worldwide Africa — our primary competitor
- 30-year MarTech platform. Their pitch: replace discounts with **experience-led rewards** through their COSMOS platform and a network of 100,000+ curated experiences globally / 6,500 partners locally.
- Their wins: McDonald's, DStv Rewards, MediaWorld, AmFam, Tommy Hilfiger, Banca di Asti, Electric Ireland.
- **Their weakness for this brief:** they are a *rewards delivery* platform — strong on what to give consumers, but **light on the deep qualitative consumer understanding** required to design a loyalty programme that genuinely changes behaviour for a brand entering a new SA category. They will pitch a turnkey rewards engine. We pitch the strategic insight foundation that the engine should sit on.

### Truth (Cromhout)
- Loyalty consultancy — they write the whitepaper, run the SA Loyalty Awards, and consult on loyalty strategy.
- **Their weakness for this brief:** consultancy-heavy, not insights-native. They produce frameworks; we produce living consumer evidence from Africa.

### How CMR Africa wins (our wedge)
We are not competing on rewards delivery (TLC's game) or on consultancy frameworks (Truth's game). We compete on the **consumer truth** that should sit *underneath* the loyalty programme architecture. The pitch: **"Before you spend R5–R20m building a rewards engine, spend a fraction on understanding the actual humans you want to keep. We'll do that, design the strategic blueprint, then partner with whichever rewards platform fits — including TLC if it's right."** This positions us as upstream of TLC and Truth, not in competition with them. Critically, because Naresh wants three quotes, we don't need to pretend to be a rewards-tech house. We can win by being the *insights and strategy* lane he didn't know he needed first.

## 5. THE STRATEGIC IDEA — WORKING TITLE: **"HOMECLUB"**

> **HomeClub by Leroy Merlin: South Africa's first home, lifestyle and movement coalition rewards ecosystem. Where every project, every weekend run, and every coffee builds the home you actually want to live in.**

### The strategic insight
South African consumers don't think about their home in isolation from how they live in it. The garden is where the kids and the dog play (Decathlon territory). The lounge is where the coffee gets drunk and the chats happen (Mugg & Bean territory). The home gym is half-DIY, half-fitness (LM × Decathlon). A loyalty programme that only rewards the *purchase of bricks* misses 80% of how a customer actually thinks about home. **Home is a verb in South Africa, not a noun.**

### The three pillars
1. **BUILD it (Leroy Merlin)** — earn HomeBucks on every spend, with project-based bonus categories (paint, garden, kitchen renovation, energy/water resilience).
2. **LIVE in it (Mugg & Bean and future F&B partners)** — coffee, family meals, the rituals of home life.
3. **MOVE in it (Decathlon)** — fitness, outdoor, kids' play. The "home extends to your weekend" promise.

Each member earns one currency (**HomeBucks**) across all three brands and can redeem against any of them.

### The mechanic that makes it sticky
**The "Project Engine" (CMR Africa's signature contribution).** When a customer scans a project barcode in the LM app — say "Bathroom Renovation" — the app sets a 90-day project window. Every spend during that window across all three coalition partners earns **2× HomeBucks** AND triggers a personalised content drip (videos, contractor matches via KANDUA, design inspo). At the end of the project, the customer is invited to share a before/after — which becomes earned-media content for LM and qualitative data for CMR Africa to feed back to the brand.

**Why this is genius:**
- Solves LM's data problem (they get to see *intent*, not just transactions).
- Solves Naresh's loyalty problem (it's not a discount card, it's a project companion).
- Solves CMR Africa's value problem (we run the project insights layer as ongoing service revenue, not a one-off study).

### Tier architecture (initial draft — will refine post store visit)
- **NEST** (free, entry) — sign up, earn 1% back as HomeBucks, app access, monthly DIY workshops, birthday coffee at Mugg & Bean.
- **HOME** (free, mid — unlocked by activity, not spend; e.g. complete one project + 3 workshops) — 2% back, free Coastal Hire tool day per quarter, priority booking on LM workshops, 10% off Decathlon kids' range.
- **HOMESTEAD** (paid R299/year OR R7,500 annual spend across coalition) — 4% back, extended 5-year warranty (mirrors Leroy & Moi+), free design consultation, KANDUA Pro contractor matching, Decathlon premium tier, Mugg & Bean priority seating, exclusive HomeStead Days (similar to Pro Xtra week).

### The mystery shopper layer (Naresh's second ask, woven in)
We design a **rolling mystery shopper programme** where members of CMR Africa's panel shop LM stores quarterly using a creative visual report format (think: sketchbook-meets-Instagram-carousel rather than a 60-page Word doc). This generates:
- Service consistency benchmarking (Greenstone vs. Little Falls vs. Boksburg vs. Alberton vs. Cornubia).
- Mystery shopper *as content* — visual stories that LM's marketing team can repurpose internally and even use in store-team training.
- Direct linkage between mystery shop scores and HomeClub member NPS (a unique CMR Africa methodology — we cross-reference mystery panel insight with real HomeClub member sentiment, which neither TLC nor Truth can deliver because they don't have a panel).

## 8. FIELD FINDINGS — LEE'S STORE VISIT (21 APRIL 2026)

Lee visited the Leroy Merlin Greenstone store on 21 April 2026 and purchased a product. Documented via 17 photographs. The findings are significant enough that they materially reshape sections 3, 5 and 7 of this document — see implications at the end of this section.

### Environment
- Store is **visually polished and consistent**: LM triangle branding clean, entrance staffed and welcoming, aisles clean, lighting well-executed (skylights + LED linear fixtures), store layout intuitive.
- **Emotional retail theatre is already present** — flower-and-plant entrance zone with neon signage ("Electric Butterfly Flowers", "#PetalPowered", "Pick me UP!"), Edison bulb festoon lighting, art-directed muffin wall mural in the Mugg & Bean zone, playful tone on promo copy ("Fix your 'I'll fix it later' this April"). This means HomeClub's brand tone can match LM's playfulness rather than fight it.
- Two staff visible at entrance in LM green aprons (good front-of-house culture). Contractor-looking shopper observed in reflective vest.
- Rear of store has large **outdoor Building Materials yard** (Image 14 — clearance zone visible, palletised stock, rolling trolleys).

### Pricing, signage and the de facto loyalty scheme
- **Pricing colour code is binary and consistent:** green edge-banding = normal "1st Price" (e.g. Image 9 yellow "1ST PRICE" tag), red = promotional price. Both red hanging danglers and white-with-red-accent shelf-edge tickets.
- **CRITICAL FINDING:** Every single promotional sign in the store carries the line *"ALL PROMOTIONS ARE EXCLUSIVE TO LOYALTY MEMBERS"* (visible on Images 5, 6, 7, 8, 9, 12, 16 — across bathroom, tiles, carpentry, garden, and the April BOGO campaign).

- **BUT:** there are no points, no tiers, no rewards, no earn/burn, no personalisation, no retention layer, no coalition. It is being used purely as a price-gating mechanism. The transaction and identity data is being captured at every till but is not being monetised downstream.

This is the local equivalent of France's 12-month Leroy & Moi no-receipt return. **Implication:** the returns promise is *tierable* — HOMESTEAD members could get 6 or 12 months to match the French parent brand, turning a uniform policy into a loyalty differentiator.

## 8.1 IMPLICATIONS — HOW THE FIELD VISIT CHANGES THE PITCH

Three findings genuinely reshape the proposition:

### Finding 1: This is an activation pitch, not a build pitch.
**Previous framing:** *"Build Leroy Merlin SA a Vitality-grade loyalty programme."*
**New framing:** *"Leroy Merlin already runs a loyalty programme. It's called my Home Card. It captures identity and transaction data at every till. It has brand equity, signage, and a registered user base. But 95% of the value sits dormant. HomeClub activates what you've already built — at a fraction of the cost of a greenfield build."*

**Why this matters for Naresh:** greenfield loyalty programmes are a CapEx/OpEx conversation requiring CEO and CFO sign-off, R5–20m budgets, multi-year ROI cases. Activation of existing infrastructure is a much easier internal sell — Naresh can frame it as "we are finally extracting value from an asset we already own" rather than "please fund a new system." For a treasury manager moving into customer insight, this framing makes him look immediately strategic.

### Finding 2: The partner ecosystem is already live, just not unified.
**Kandua (contractors), Coastal Hire (tools), Mugg & Bean (F&B), supplier brands (Sensea, Naterial, Luxens, Macneil, Triumph) are all co-branded in-store today.** Our original pitch treated Decathlon and Mugg & Bean as the coalition. The real coalition is already 5–7 partners deep, operating in-store, with existing commercial relationships. We just need to thread a single HomeBucks currency through the whole network. Decathlon becomes the obvious *external* expansion once internal partners are unified.

**Lee's specific insight** from the field: **HomeBucks should be earnable and spendable against services, not just products.**
- Earn HomeBucks for booking a Kandua contractor through LM.
- Redeem HomeBucks for a Coastal Hire tool rental day.
- Redeem HomeBucks against Cutting & Edging Workshop piece-pricing.
- Redeem HomeBucks against installation fees.
- Earn bonus HomeBucks on eco-product purchases (water / energy resilience).

This is the single most defensible differentiator vs. a TLC COSMOS platform pitch — **TLC thinks in rewards categories, we think in customer journeys**. A homeowner doing a bathroom renovation doesn't buy products then rewards — they buy a *project*, which is tiles + adhesive + a toilet + a plumber + a tool rental + a coffee while they figure out grout colour. HomeClub earns and burns across all of that.

### Finding 3: Experiential rewards are the cheapest, highest-perceived-value lever.
Naresh mentioned House and Garden shows, home expos, affiliated parties. This is directly TLC's playbook (replace cash discounts with experiential rewards) — but LM has a *better* supply of these than TLC does, because LM is already in conversation with these event organisers as a sponsor and exhibitor. Event tickets, VIP access to expos, workshops with celebrity renovators, preview nights for new product launches — all are near-zero-cost to LM (media trade) but premium-perceived to members.

**What to build into HomeClub:**
- **"HomeStead Days"** — quarterly member-only events in-store (after-hours access, 15% off everything, demo workshops, free wine & snacks). Mirrors Home Depot's Pro Xtra Week.
- **Event redemption tier** — VIP tickets to Decorex, Homemakers Expo, Design Joburg, House and Garden Show, Good Food & Wine Show exclusively for HomeClub members.
- **LM-curated masterclasses** — "Paint a feature wall with a celebrity designer," "Build a raised herb bed with [influencer]" — content Bra Bens can produce.

## 8.2 TACTICAL IDEAS THE FIELD VISIT INSPIRED (FOR PHASE 2)

Small, executable, high-impact:

1. **The coral "2× HomeBucks" price ticket.** Leave green (1st price) and red (promo) untouched. Add a third coral-orange ticket for "Project Bonus Items" during a member's active project window. Zero ops disruption, huge behavioural cue.
2. **"Scan the aisle" treasure hunts.** QR-coded aisle signs that unlock weekly mini-challenges (50–150 HB per completion). No new hardware required beyond printed stickers.
3. **Tierable 3-month returns.** Keep base at 3 months. HOME = 6 months. HOMESTEAD = 12 months (matches French parent-brand promise).
4. **Kandua HomeBucks flow-through.** 5% of Kandua contractor booking value credited back to the customer in HomeBucks (funded from Kandua margin share, not LM).
5. **Coastal Hire "free tool day" redemption.** Standard redemption option (~800 HB) that LM customers can use for a weekend project. Makes HomeBucks *useful*, not a locked savings account.
6. **"Water Warrior" / "Energy Warrior" badges** for members who purchase eco-products (Maldives low-flush toilets, Naterial artificial grass which saves water, Luxens insulating paints, etc.). Ties directly to Dufourq's three public missions.
7. **Mugg & Bean "Project Break" promo.** While you're in an active project window, get a free filter coffee at Mugg & Bean each weekend — a small, emotional, memorable perk that creates store-visit frequency.
8. **Post-checkout HomeBucks receipt moment.** At self-checkout, after member identification, show an animated "You earned X HomeBucks today" screen with progress toward next tier. Turns the till into a reward moment.
9. **Workshop attendance → HomeBucks.** The Cutting & Edging Workshop and DIY workshops currently don't feed any loyalty signal. Attendance + completion of a DIY workshop = 100 HB. Creates a behavioural loop.
10. **Seasonal coalition campaigns.** "April = Fix Your 'I'll Fix It Later'" already exists as LM copy. HomeClub layer: members who complete a project in April earn a 500 HB April Bonus and automatic entry into a draw for a R50k home renovation consultation.
